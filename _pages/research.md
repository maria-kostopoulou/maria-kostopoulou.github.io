## Publications & Accepted Papers

<details>
<summary><strong>Valuing a Reduction in the Risk of Non-Fatal Cancer</strong> <em>(under review, Environmental and Resource Economics)</em> with D. Herrera-Araujo, H. Andersson, D. Dussaux & O. Byström. OECD Environment Working Papers, 2026.</summary>

<br>
<p>Cancer is associated with various environmental contaminants and chemicals, including heavy metals, pesticides, as well as foodborne and waterborne pathogens. Many of these hazards are subject to regulation, or may be considered for regulation, to reduce exposure and prevent human health risks. In addition to the well-known significant mortality risks, cancer also causes substantial morbidity, including debilitating symptoms and treatment side effects that severely impact patients' quality of life. Despite this, there has been no comprehensive study of the morbidity of cancer, making it difficult to fully assess the broader burden and economic implications of cancer. This paper is part of the series of large-scale willingness-to-pay (WTP) studies resulting from the Surveys on Willingness-to-pay to Avoid Negative Chemicals-Related Health Effects (SWACHE) project that intends to improve the basis for doing cost benefit analyses of chemicals management options and environmental policies in general. The present paper details a stated preference survey estimating the value of avoiding a case of non-fatal cancer (VNF). The SWACHE non-fatal cancer survey was fielded in 10 countries: Canada, Denmark, Estonia, Japan, Mexico, Norway, Slovenia, Spain, the United Kingdom and the United States. In each country, a sample of approximately 1 200 individuals representative of the general population was collected and empirically analysed. Based on this survey, the mean VNF is USD2023 Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) 883 000. The VNF ranges from USD2023 PPP 559 000 in Mexico to USD2023 PPP 1 266 000 in the United States.</p>
